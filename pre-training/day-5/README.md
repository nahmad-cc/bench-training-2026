# Day 5: Titanic Dataset Analysis

## Most Surprising Finding

The biggest surprise was how much **class mattered**. First class had a 63% survival rate, but third class only 24%. 

The second surprise was gender - **74% of women survived but only 19% of men**. This "women and children first" policy was real and it shows in the data. Even in 3rd class, where conditions were terrible, 50% of women survived vs only 13.5% of men.

## What I'd Investigate Next

1. **Family size impact** - Did families with more people have better or worse survival rates? Maybe traveling with someone helped your chances.

2. **Ticket price vs survival** - I bet people who paid more for fancy tickets were closer to lifeboats. How does fare amount correlate with survival?

3. **Age cutoff for "children"** - I used <18 as children, but maybe the crew treated <15 year olds differently. Where's the actual cutoff in the data?

4. **Cabin location** - Did people in specific cabin areas have better survival rates? The ones with Cabin data were mostly first class, so that's survival bias.

5. **Names/titles analysis** - Extract title (Mr., Mrs., Miss) and see if that improved survival prediction. Titles might tell us about family relationships we don't see directly.

If I had more time, I'd definitely dig into the cabin data more carefully and check if cabin location on the ship made a difference.
